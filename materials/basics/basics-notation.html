Algebraic Notation
Reading a move

Every move is written as a piece letter followed by the destination square. Pawns have no letter — just the square they move to.

  • Nf3 — the Knight moves to f3.
  • e4 — a pawn moves to e4 (pawns carry no letter).
  • Bb5 — the Bishop moves to b5.
Piece letters: K = King, Q = Queen, R = Rook, B = Bishop, N = Knight (N, because K is taken). Pawns have no letter.

Pawn captures name the file the pawn came from: exd5 means “the e-file pawn captures on d5”.
A whole game in writing

Moves are numbered, with White's move first, then Black's. This famous four-move finish is the “Scholar's Mate”:

1. e4   e5
2. Bc4   Nc6
3. Qh5   Nf6??
4. Qxf7#   (checkmate — White wins)

Being able to read notation lets you follow any game, study from any book, and record your own games to learn from them later.
