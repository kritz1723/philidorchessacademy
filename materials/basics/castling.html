8
Castling
What is castling?

The only move where two pieces move at once. The King moves 2 squares towards a Rook; the Rook jumps to the adjacent square. Counts as a single move.

♚ Kingside — O-O
King e1→g1. Rook h1→f1. King tucked safely behind f2, g2, h2 pawns.
♛ Queenside — O-O-O
King e1→c1. Rook a1→d1. King travels further; rook becomes very active on d1.
All 7 rules — every condition must be true
Key piece Target square Danger square
1
The King must not have moved
King: unmoved
If the King has ever moved this game — even one square and back — castling rights are permanently lost.
King e1 unmoved — O-O still available
2
The Rook being used must not have moved
Rook: unmoved
Each rook's castling right is independent. If the h1 rook moved, White can still castle queenside if the a1 rook is unmoved.
Both rooks unmoved — both rights available
3
No pieces between King and Rook
Path: clear
Every square between King and Rook must be completely empty. Kingside needs f1, g1 clear. Queenside needs b1, c1, d1 clear.
f1 and g1 empty — kingside path clear
4
King moves 2 squares — Rook jumps to adjacent square
Mechanics
The King moves exactly 2 squares towards the rook. The rook jumps to the square immediately next to the king. Kingside: K→g1, R→f1. Queenside: K→c1, R→d1.
After O-O: King g1, Rook f1
5
King cannot be in check when castling
No check
If the King is currently in check, castling is illegal — even if all other conditions are met.
Black rook on e8 checks e1 — cannot castle
6
King cannot pass through an attacked square
Safe path
The King cannot cross a square controlled by an enemy piece. Kingside: f1 must be safe. Queenside: d1 must be safe.
If f1 is attacked, O-O is illegal
7
King cannot land on an attacked square
Safe landing
The square the King lands on must also be safe. If g1 is attacked, kingside castling is illegal.
Black rook controls g1 — O-O illegal
