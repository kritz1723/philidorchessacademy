Supported Piece
What is a supported piece?

A supported (or defended) piece is one that has at least one other friendly piece protecting it. If an opponent captures it, your protecting piece immediately recaptures — so they lose material in the exchange.

Always check before capturing: is the piece I'm about to take supported? If yes, is the trade profitable based on point values?
Examples of supported pieces
♙Pawn supported by pawn
White pawn on e4 is defended by the pawn on d3. If Black captures exd3, White recaptures with dxe4 or another piece. The pawn on e4 is not free.
♘Knight supported by bishop
White knight on d5 is defended by the bishop on b3. If Black captures the knight with Nxd5, White recaptures with Bxd5. The knight costs Black a piece to take.
Why supporting your pieces matters
  • Undefended pieces get captured for free — always ensure your pieces are protected or plan to move them before they can be taken
  • Supported pieces can advance boldly — a piece that is defended can go to aggressive squares without fear of being captured for free
  • Piece coordination — pieces that support each other work as a team; a lone piece is vulnerable
  • Hanging pieces lose games — always scan the board for your own undefended pieces after every opponent move
